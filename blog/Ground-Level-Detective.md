# The Ground-Level Detective: How to Spot Storm Damage Without a Ladder

While it’s the big, flashy storms with their rolling thunderheads and golf-ball-sized hailstones that make the evening news, it’s often the repetitive, smaller showers that do the most insidious damage to a Nebraska home.

At Roof Rite Exteriors, we’ve often seen homeowners overlook these minor thunderstorms because *"the roof looked fine from the driveway."*

The truth? Hail damage isn’t always obvious, and left ignored, small bruises on your shingles can turn into major leaks by winter. The good news is that you don’t need to risk climbing up a tall ladder to find out if that storm left a mark.

---

## The Three-Item Checklist That Could Save You Thousands

Before you call your insurance agent, take a quick five-minute walk around your home with this ground-level detective checklist:

### 1. Inspect the Gutters and Downspouts

Your gutter system acts like a magnet for storm debris.

* **Check the metal:** Look closely at the aluminum sections—are there fresh, round dimples or deep dents?
* **Look at the ground:** Take a look right beneath your downspout extensions. If you see a heavy accumulation of dark, coarse sand, those are shingle granules.

> **Why this matters:** Impacts from hailstones literally knock these protective granules loose, washing them right into your gutters and leaving your shingles highly vulnerable to UV and water damage.

### 2. Scan the Siding and Window Trim

Propelled by ferocious summer winds, hailstones in Nebraska rarely fall perfectly straight down. Wind usually drives the hail at an angle, so examine the side of your house that faced the brunt of the storm.

* Look for cracks, chips, or holes in vinyl siding.
* Check for dents in your aluminum trim and window flashing.

If the impact was strong enough to damage your siding, your roof likely took an even harder hit.

### 3. Look at the Surrounding Yard

Nature leaves great clues. Check your deck, patio furniture, and landscaping:

* Do you see shredded, split, or heavily stripped leaves scattered everywhere?
* Are plastic lawn fixtures, fence posts, or exterior light covers cracked or broken?

Damaged yardscapes are a clear baseline indicator that the hail in your neighborhood was dense and hard enough to compromise building materials.

By spotting these subtle red flags early, you can stay ahead of hidden leaks and ensure you have the evidence you need if you decide to move forward with a professional inspection.

## Beat the Clock! The Insurance Reality

If your home has been hit by a summer storm, finding the damage is only one part of the equation—you also have to factor in the ticking clock of insurance claim windows.

Many homeowners assume they can wait until a rainy day—or a leaking ceiling on a rainy day—to call their insurance company. Unfortunately, waiting too long can be an incredibly expensive mistake.

| The Insurance Timeline | What You Need to Know |
| --- | --- |
| **The One-Year Rule** | While every insurance provider has slightly different policies, most standard homeowner insurance contracts require you to file a storm damage claim within **one year (365 days)** of the actual storm date. |
| **The Danger of Waiting** | If a severe hailstorm hits your neighborhood in July, but you don't notice a roof leak until the following August, your insurance company may deny the claim simply because the filing window closed. At that point, you are entirely responsible for the cost of repairs or a full replacement. |
| **Proactive Documentation** | Insurance adjustments go much more smoothly when you have clear, immediate evidence. A professional roof inspection shortly after a storm gives you **time-stamped proof** of the damage. This ties the issues directly to the specific summer storm, leaving no room for the insurer to argue that the wear and tear happened over time. |

*Not sure if the storm damaged your roof? The findings of a professional roof inspection are great to keep in your permanent home maintenance and upkeep records.*
